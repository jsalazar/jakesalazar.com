The U-M School of Education prepares teachers who are capable of — and committed to — providing a powerful education for all students in our world. We believe that public education is a vital element of a just society.

Our teacher education programs, led by award-winning faculty who excel at both teaching and research, are ranked among the top in the nation. It is a model that is sought after by many certifying institutions.

As a practice-based program that is rich with resources to support teacher education, the U-M School of Education provides opportunities for students to work in classrooms every semester, under the guidance of faculty, staff, mentors, and colleagues. Each part of the program is designed to prepare students as they learn and grow into the profession.

Students also work closely with a cohort of their peers to connect challenging coursework with guided experience in schools. The cohort model provides a lasting network of colleagues and allows students to share ideas, practice skills, and enjoy the support of a community.

We offer both bachelor of arts and bachelor of science degrees. Upon completion of the degree, qualified students are recommended to the Michigan Department of Education for teacher certification.

BECOME AN ELEMENTARY TEACHER

Undergraduate elementary teacher education is a four-term program that prepares students to teach kindergarten through eighth grade. Elementary teachers have the unique opportunity to teach literacy, mathematics, science, and social studies in ways that support the development of the whole child. This approach seeks to create a learning environment that supports a strong understanding of children as sense-makers who are capable of developing deep understanding of content across disciplines.

Students in the elementary teacher education curriculum also have the opportunity to earn an endorsement in teaching English as a Second Language.

BECOME A SECONDARY TEACHER

Undergraduate secondary teacher education is a three-term program that prepares students to teach grades six through twelve. Secondary instruction provides students with the opportunity to teach subjects about which they have a deep understanding. The program also offers the option to add certification to the degree a student is pursuing in another school or college at the University of Michigan.

Secondary teacher education students have the opportunity to earn an additional certification to teach in International Baccalaureate (IB) Programmes around the world and/or an endorsement in teaching English as a Second Language.

APPLYING TO OUR PROGRAMS

The U-M School of Education bachelor’s programs begin in the third year. Any teacher education students may transition to the School from other schools on the Ann Arbor campus or transfer from other colleges and universities. Secondary teacher certification students also have the option to stay in their U-M school or college. Whether you are already on campus or considering a transfer, please contact us early so that we can help you plan your coursework.

We accept first-year undergraduate students in a preferred admissions program. These students complete their first two years at another school at the University of Michigan (usually the College of Literature, Science, and the Arts), while being advised by the School prior to transferring to our program at the beginning of the third year. Other U-M students apply to the School during their first two years and formally transfer as juniors.

ALREADY A U-M STUDENT?

Expand your career options by adding teacher certification. If you’re a U-M student in another unit, such as the College of Literature, Science, and the Arts, we have options that allow you to earn your degree while also earning a teaching certificate. Students interested in music education must apply directly through the School of Music, Theatre & Dance.

ALREADY HAVE A BACHELOR’S DEGREE?

You can earn our recommendation to the Michigan Department of Education for teacher certification through our post-baccalaureate option or by attaining a master’s degree in one year through our Master of Arts with Teacher Certification program.
